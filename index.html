Crime and Detective

If you're looking for a show that offers both the thrill of solving crimes with a lighthearted and familial team of federal agents, NCIS might be just the show you you. Each episode centers around a different crime. The characters are relatable, fun, and balance out the darkness inherently associated with violent crime. While only for teen and adult audiences, you are less likely to be uncomfortable or triggered watching this show as you will with many others in the genre.

A fan of the darker side of things? More interested in the psychological side of crime? Then try Criminal Minds, and its reboot Criminal Minds: Evolution. Dark and disturbing enough to make some of the actors leave the show, this analyzes heinous serial crimes from the perspective of a team of FBI profilers. Follow along as they create a profile for every twisted mind they encounter to catch criminals. Episodes alternate between hiding the criminal to showing what they're like while still on the loose. Despite it's gracious ratings, this will offer chilling insights into psychopathic minds.

There are plenty more good shows out there, including... Prodigal Son, The Equalizer, Sherlock, Mindhunter, CSI, Forensic Files, Law and Order, Murder She Wrote, Miami Vice, Hawaii 5-O, Without a Trace, Hinterland, You, and Peaky Blinders.

Why not these shows? Realistically, I don't watch as many shows as I thought I did to recommend them. These three are highly rated and offer a wide variety of moods and content between them, making them great places to start and discover what you like most within the crime and detective genres.

It's in the nature of this genre to include graphic, disturbing, and violent elements. Please be aware of what types of content may appear before watching the show. If you are uncomfortable with violence, blood, sex and nudity, or are triggered by certain incidents, do your research prior to watching. Some shows will not be as graphic as others. If you take the time to look, you can find ways to enjoy the genre without compromising or making yourself more uncomfortable than watching crime requires you to be.
